UK court denies release of substantial funds for Mallya's legal fees
January 12, 2021
Naitik Raysoni
One-liner: A UK court refused to sanction the release of substantial sums held with the Court Funds Office as part of bankruptcy proceedings against Vijay Mallya by a consortium of Indian banks, led by the SBI.
One-liner: A UK court refused to sanction the release of substantial sums held with the Court Funds Office as part of bankruptcy proceedings against Vijay Mallya by a consortium of Indian banks, led by the SBI.

Zoom in: Judge Sebastian Prentis, who presided over a remote hearing at the Insolvency and Companies Court (ICC) of the High Court in London, concluded that Mallya had failed to provide basic information required for such a validation order.
  • However, he agreed to allow sufficient funds to cover an appeal hearing scheduled in the bankruptcy case for Wednesday
Judge raises questions: "As to the urgency of this application, I remain puzzled," the judge noted, as he raised questions about updated evidence on full disclosure of assets at Mallya's disposal, including jewellery and high-value luxury cars that were listed as being owned by him.

Seeks approval: The former Kingfisher Airlines chief sought approval to use £2.8mn from the estimated £2.9mn held by the court under the UK's Insolvency Act 1986.
  • Mallya's counsel, Philip Marshall, argued that his client needed urgent access to funds to avert a high risk of him losing his legal representation
